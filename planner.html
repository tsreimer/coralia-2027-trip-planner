Planning notes
What to know before you book
Arrive 1 night early
You arrive in Labuan Bajo July 29 to board Coralia on July 30. Use the evening to check in, explore the waterfront, and run any last-minute gear checks before the liveaboard departs the next morning.
Book early — July is peak season
July is peak dry season and the most popular month to visit Labuan Bajo. AYANA and TA'AKTANA in particular sell out well in advance. Book as soon as you've confirmed your stopover city and flights — ideally 6–9 months ahead.
Airport is 5–15 min away
Komodo Airport (LBJ) is small and very close to all resorts — most offer a complimentary shuttle. Plataran requires a short boat transfer from the harbour, so allow an extra 30 minutes on departure day when heading back to the airport.
Coralia briefing & gear check
The Coralia team typically arranges a pre-departure briefing on the evening of July 29. Your resort concierge can arrange transport to the harbour (Labuan Bajo Port). Confirm pick-up time with the Coralia crew after booking.
Marriott Bonvoy at TA'AKTANA
If you hold Marriott Bonvoy points, TA'AKTANA is bookable with points. An overwater sea villa typically requires ~50,000–80,000 points per night. This can significantly offset the premium pricing — worth checking your balance.
Indonesia visa — 30 days on arrival
US citizens receive a 30-day visa on arrival at LBJ airport for $35 USD. No pre-approval needed. Your total trip (resort + liveaboard) is 12 days — well within the limit. Bring cash USD for the visa fee on arrival.
Our recommendation Book AYANA Komodo Waecicu Beach for 1 night (Jul 29). Best value, on-site dive club to test gear, and the rooftop bar delivers an unforgettable sunset before you board Coralia. If AYANA is unavailable, TA'AKTANA's overwater sea villa is worth every dollar for a special occasion.
