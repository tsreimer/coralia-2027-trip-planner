5-Day Highlights
📍 Day-by-Day Ideas
  • Day 1–2: Gyeongbokgung Palace, Bukchon Hanok Village, Insadong, Changdeokgung & Secret Garden
  • Day 3: DMZ & JSA (Joint Security Area) day trip — book in advance
  • Day 4: Hongdae, Mapo, Sinchon — street food, art, nightlife
  • Day 5: Myeongdong shopping, N Seoul Tower (Namsan), Gangnam (optional)
🍜 Dining Tips
  • CatchTable app — essential for booking popular spots (Oncheonjip, Mongtan, etc.)
  • Popular areas: Insadong for traditional, Itaewon for international, Mapo for trendy spots
  • Must-try: Korean BBQ, bibimbap, jjajangmyeon, tteokbokki, Korean fried chicken
🚇 Getting Around
  • T-Money card — subway & bus, purchase at any station or convenience store
  • Kakao Taxi — reliable app-based taxi for late nights, works in English
  • Naver Maps — better than Google for Seoul walking directions
  • Subway: Extensive, cheap, signs in English — fastest way around the city
⚠️ Things to Know
  • Currency: Korean Won (KRW) — most places accept cards; carry some cash for markets
  • Language: Google Translate camera mode useful; most tourist areas have English signage
  • Weather: Late July is hot and humid (~30°C/86°F) with occasional rain — pack light layers
